COIR PITH

Types


Unwashed

Unwashed Coir Pith typically has a high EC and is therefore great for the growth flowers typically.


Washed

Washed coir pith has a low EC and does not block the osmosis of certain sensitive plants. Also, this means that you may add the salts and chemicals necessaery to the growth of only your plant. This is typically great for vegetables.


Buffered

This is a chemically enhanced version of coir pith; the levels of sodium and pottasium are lowered and calcium is increased to help more sensitive plants such as strawberries. This is used typically in fruit farms.


Chips

Coir husk is cut into cubes called chips and can be bought as washed, unwashed or buffered. It is also a very light and spongy materialt that has a good water retention material. They are typically mixed with coir pith in a certain ratio (60-40, 50-50, etc). These are useful as they create air pockets and provide support to the roots amidst all the loose coir pith.


Partially Decomposed

Coir pith is partially decomposed and made nutritionally available over a period of 5-6 weeks. This means that the farmer doesn't have to give any additional decomposed material and nutritional supplemens to the plants over the first six months.

The other types of coir pith do not decompose as quickly due to their high lignin content. Using our own unique and proprietary technology, we break down the lignin of the coir pith, allowing it to decompose and be used as nutrition for plants rather than jusr a medium for the nutrition. However, this reduces its lifespan to 1 year after which it can be used as manure.
